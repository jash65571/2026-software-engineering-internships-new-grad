# Referral Outreach Templates

Referrals raise your odds of getting an interview. Use these templates as starting points and edit them so they sound like you.

## Cold LinkedIn message to an employee at the company

Subject / first line: short and specific.

```
Hi [Name],

I came across the [Role title] opening on the [Company] careers page and noticed you work on [team or product they actually work on]. I would love your read on whether my background is a fit before I apply.

Quick context: [1 sentence on what you do or studied + one project or result].

If you have a few minutes this week, I can send a short version of my resume and the role link. No pressure if the timing is bad.

Thanks either way,
[Your name]
[LinkedIn / portfolio]
```

## Email to an alum or past coworker

```
Hi [Name],

Hope you have been well at [Company]. I am applying to the [Role title] role and your name came up as someone who works there.

Would you be open to a quick chat or to passing my resume to the hiring manager if it looks like a fit? Happy to share a tailored resume and the role link.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

## Follow-up after 5 to 7 business days

```
Hi [Name],

Just floating this back to the top. Totally fine if now is not a good time. If easier, I can keep this short: one paragraph about my background and the role link. Let me know what works.

Thanks,
[Your name]
```

## Tips

- Personalize the first line. Show you read their LinkedIn.
- One ask per message. Don't ask for a referral and a coffee chat in the same note.
- Attach a tailored resume, not a generic one.
- Don't follow up more than twice.
- Thank them whether they help or not.

## Finding the right person

- Look for engineers on the same team as the role.
- Avoid messaging recruiters first if you want a real referral.
- If the person is too senior, find someone two or three levels closer to the role.
