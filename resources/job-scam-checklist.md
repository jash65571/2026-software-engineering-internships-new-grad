# Job Scam Checklist

Fake job posts are common in software engineering. Use this checklist before applying or replying.

## Strong signals it is a scam

- Recruiter only contacts you on Telegram, WhatsApp, or Signal.
- The "company" email uses Gmail, Outlook, or a free domain.
- You are hired without any technical interview.
- You are asked to buy your own equipment and reimburse later.
- You are asked for bank details, SSN, or ID before any signed offer.
- The salary is much higher than the market for the role.
- The job description is copied from a real company but the contact is not.
- The apply link goes to a Google Form or random Notion page instead of the company's careers page.

## Yellow flags

- Vague role title with no real tech stack listed.
- Company website is brand new or has almost no real content.
- LinkedIn page for the company has fewer than 50 employees but claims a massive valuation.
- Recruiter's LinkedIn has no posts, no history, and only stock images.
- "Urgent" hiring pressure to skip checks.

## Steps before responding

1. Search the company name + "scam" or "fake job."
2. Visit the careers page directly. Find the role listed there.
3. Search the recruiter on LinkedIn and check tenure.
4. Confirm the email domain matches the company's real domain.
5. Never send ID documents until you have a signed offer letter.

## If you already replied

- Stop the conversation.
- Do not click any new links.
- Block the contact.
- Report the post to the platform (LinkedIn, Indeed, etc.).
- Report identity fraud to your local authority if you shared sensitive info.
